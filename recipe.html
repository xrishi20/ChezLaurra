Simple Oatmeal Pancakes

This tart is one of my favorite desserts from childhood and the one my mother pampers me with most often during the summer. After several attempts at various recipes and modulating the ingredients, I came to the recipe below, which is neither 100% clafoutis (because it does not contain milk), nor pandispan (because the top is much thinner). At the same time the countertop is fluffier and more airy than that of a clafoutis, but not as tall as that of the pandispanâ€¦ in the end I think I could call it "clafouprajiturique"? Basic Info
  • Yield 8 Pancakes
  • Prep Time 5 min.
  • Cook Time 15 min.
  • Total Time 20 min.

Ingredients
  • 200 g flour
  • 100 g of sugar
  • maple syrup (optional)
  • 5 eggs (egg yolk and egg white separated)
  • 4 tablespoons oil
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa
  • 1 sachet baking powder (10 g)
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 cinnamon powder
  • nuts / cashew / cranberry
Method of Preparation
  • First mix the yolks with the oil in a bowl. In another mixture flour with baking powder and salt.
  • Then beat the egg whites, using a food processor or mixer.
  • Gradually add the sugar until the composition becomes sufficiently homogeneous so that gravity no longer works on it ðŸ™‚.
  • Meanwhile, mix cocoa with cinnamon and a little water and / or maple syrup.
  • I move the composition from the robot into a container and gradually pour the yolks and then the flour over it.
  • If you want to get a brighter color, you have to put the maple syrup at the stage of incorporating the yolks, as well, little by little, before incorporating the flour.
  • I divide the composition into two parts - half a mixture with cocoa and half I leave it simple.
  • Bake-shaped lathe, in turn, a little of the simple composition, then a little of the cocoa composition.
  • Sprinkle walnuts / cashews / cranberries on top and put in the oven for about 30 minutes at 200 Â°.
